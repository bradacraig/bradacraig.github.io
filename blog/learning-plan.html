Learning Plan

What is your long term goal or career pathway?

My ultimate long term goal is a solid sustainable career in a tech related/adjacent industry. I'm not shooting for a job at meta or google, just something where I can use the skills I've developed at Dev Academy in a rewarding, interesting way. Eventually it would be great if this job had some remote work possibilities to allow me the flexibility to carry on with my musical projects as these are an important part of my life.

In the short term (6months - 1 year after course completion) I'm essentially open to absolutely anything within reason. During this time and even up to 2 years after course completion I'd like to find a job at a NZ based company and actually go into an office to do work. I feel this is important as I'd like to build my network and gain on the job experience as a junior developer before working towards a more flexible role.

Generally, I'd like to find a job that will allow me to live comfortably and allow me the opportunity to buy a house and start a family.

Describe your strengths and limitations when it comes to learning

My strengths include:

  • Positivity: I tend to have an optimistic, glass half full outlook which allows me to see the bright side of learning and to aim high and convince myself I'm able to solve a problem.
  • Determination and Perseverance: I'm driven and motivated to get the job done.
  • Curiosity and enthusiasm to learn: I’m thankful that I genuinely enjoy the learning process and look forward to developing new skills. I’m hoping that this will keep me motivated throughout the course and drive my learning journey.

My limitations Include:

  • Over excitement and over-engineering/perfectionism: I noticed this while building out the blog website in particular. I tend to get a bit carried away with a big picture idea and end up spending too much time on "nice to have but not essential" features. I need to practice better time management to get the minimum concept working first before investing time into making it perfect.
  • Fear of failure and mistakes: Mistakes are going to be made somewhere along this learning journey, and I’m not always going to get it right the first time. However, I need to be mindful of this and accept that this is all part of the process, rather than letting the fear stop me from making progress. I think the idea of breaking problems down into smaller chunks and being methodical rather than trying to speed through tasks should help me here.

What skills (non-technical core/human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

  • Time management: I'm getting better at this thanks to the pomodoro timer and time boxing, however I feel like I can still improve.
  • Self-confidence: Often I will wait to let someone go first in case I'm wrong, I'd like to work on this to be able to share first and not worry if it might not be the perfect answer.

My commitment to how I will manage my workload in this programme.

I will strive to work productively and safely for myself by:

  • Practising good time management by timing my focused work sessions with a pomodoro timer and taking frequent short breaks as to not burn myself out.
  • I will break tasks down into smaller, more manageable chunks and aim to set achievable goals for each day.
  • I will understand that there will be easy days and hard days and that this is normal. I will be grateful for when I pick something up quickly and try my best to not get disheartened when I struggle.

I will strive to work productively and safely with other learners by:

  • Understanding that everyone is learning at a different pace and as a result I can't expect to be as fast or efficient as someone who is approaching this course with lots of experience under their belt. On the other hand, it is no use becoming frustrated with someone who has less experience than me and expect everyone to understand everything from the get go. The boat rises with the ocean, so I will try my best to offer help when I can and to ask for help when I need it.
  • I will respect other learners personal culture and viewpoints.
  • I will respect learning hours and not expect everyone to keep the same schedule as me.

I will strive to work productively and safely with facilitators/community representatives by:

  • Respecting the opinions, views and advice given to me by facilitators/community representatives.
  • Respecting the time that facilitators/community representatives are giving to me and understanding that they are not available at every hour of the day.
  • Respecting the personal cultures and views of facilitators/community representatives.

When things are starting to build up and get to me I will:

  • Take a break: Leave the room, go for a walk or do some exercise. Try revisiting what is causing the build up with a fresh head.
  • Try to explain what is causing the stress/build up first to myself (write it down, speak it out loud), then to a friend or fellow learner and or facilitator.
  • Break the problem down to try and get some easy wins on the board to lift my mood.
  • Not be afraid to admit I'm having a problem and ask for help.

I will strive to seek help in a timely way by:

  1. Giving the problem an honest attempt on my own first and trying to pinpoint where I might be going wrong.
  2. Put my problem into words by asking the question on google or Chat GPT. With regards to Chat GPT I will write the prompt in a way that it will act as a tutor or mentor and help me to understand the problem I'm having.
  3. Reach out on the appropriate discord channel to see if fellow learners and or facilitators can help.
  4. DM a facilitator if I'm really struggling and need focused, specific help.

What I expect from the Facilitation team:

I expect the facilitation team to be professional, knowledgable and patient. I expect them to be friendly and convivial when appropriate and to respect the different views, strengths and limitations of all of the learners. I expect them to be able to help where and when they can, but do not expect them to know everything and be available 24/7.
