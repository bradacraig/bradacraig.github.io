Foundations Reflections

What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the core learning?

  • I notice when I slip into a fixed mindset now... This isn't necessarily a bad thing but it serves as a reminder that I might need to take a break and reevaluate.
  • I struggled to talk about the more spiritual side of myself as it's not something I really practice or think about much.
  • I work much better in the mornings and when I have a clear plan or goal for the day. I can become easily distracted and slip into procrastination if I lose focus.

What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

Values can help guide our learning as they can provide insight into how we learn and what we can focus on to improve our learning. It can guide our learning style and allow us to learn more effectively either alone or in a group. In regards to programming, they can help guide a project to make sure that our values align with the requirements of the end-user and provide goals to strive for when developing in a team.

Empathy can help us better relate and understand other people we are learning with, teachers, and even other people that support us while learning. It can help to remind us that we are not alone on this journey and that not everyone sees and experiences things the way we do. Being open to other people's experiences and emotions can enrich our learning and provide better outcomes for everyone. Empathy in programming can help us to think from the viewpoint of the end-user and to make sure they are accessible to a wide variety of users from different backgrounds or cultures.

Self-awareness is important in learning as it enables us to recognise our strengths and weaknesses and how they influence our emotions and behaviour. By demonstrating self-awareness we can regulate our emotions, empathise with others, and make better decisions as a result. In programming being self-aware can help us to grow by understanding our strengths and weaknesses and allows us to be continuously improving and striving to be competitive and the best we can be.

What has surprised you the most about the core learning?

I was surprised at how deep it got into the psychology at times. This was by no means a negative thing and the fact that it was tied in with thinking about learning how to code made it more interesting.

What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?

  • I find writing about myself quite hard in general. I definitely hear the internal voice in my head criticising and cringing when trying to put words down. Sometimes it feels difficult to put into words exactly what or how I'm feeling.
  • Trying to nail down my strengths and weaknesses was quite hard as it's not something I really think about a lot.

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

I think it is because a lot of the jobs in tech can be quite mentally demanding and having a good understanding of oneself and how you relate to others can help to make sure you don't burn out. Although a lot of the past month has been individual work, most of the jobs will require close collaboration and teamwork. Understanding and developing these core "soft" skills can help us to work better in these environments.

Does the time you spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practice programming instead? Justify your answer.

I don't think the core study is a waste of time, I'll be honest and say it's not exactly my favourite part of the course but I certainly understand its value when it comes to learning and practicing programming due to how communication-heavy the discipline is. I felt like I still had plenty of time to practice programming.
