Te Whare Tapa Wha

Walls

Activities

Taha Tinana (Physical Wellbeing)

Mon, Wed, Fri: Strength, Core and Mobility workout Tue, Thu, Sat/Sun: Run or Bike

Taha Whānau (Family Wellbeing)

Catch up with grandparents once a week for a walk and dinner. Dinner with my partner Emily each night, no phones or TV. Be present.

Taha Hinengaro (Emotional and Mental Wellbeing)

Guided meditation before starting work. Reaching out to Emily and explaining if I’m feeling stressed or burned out. Combine this with exercise regime listed above.

Taha Wairua (Spiritual Wellbeing)

Express gratitude for the natural world we are blessed with every day. Take time to listen to the wind, feel the sun, look out to the horizon.

Whenua (Interconnections to the land and environment)

Take time to feel a phyiscal connection with the land. Spend time in the garden, feel the grass. Get down to the local beach and feel the sand. Get down to Opoutere when time allows to go surfing. Full immersion in water for the ultimate connection.
