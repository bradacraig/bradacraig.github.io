What Is Emotional Intelligence?

Emotional Intelligence (EQ): Emotional Intelligence, or EQ, is the ability to recognise, understand, manage, and express emotions effectively. It involves skills like self-awareness, empathy, and social awareness, enabling us to navigate emotional situations, build relationships, and make good decisions.

Difference between EQ and IQ

EQ: Refers to emotional abilities, social skills, and understanding the emotions of yourself and others. It includes empathy, emotional regulation, and motivation. It is useful for navigating interpersonal relationships and conflict resolution.

IQ: Is a measure of cognitive and intellectual skills like logical reasoning, problem-solving, and the ability to retain knowledge. It assesses abilities related to learning, understanding, and applying information to tasks

Importance Of EQ

Finding Enjoyment and Success in Life: Strong emotional Intelligence has been linked to positive life outcomes, job success, relationship satisfaction, stress tolerance, and overall well-being.

Workplace Success: A robust EQ is crucial for effective leadership, teamwork, conflict resolution, and communication. People with high EQ are often great leaders and team players due to their ability to understand, empathise, and connect with others.

My Own Experience With Emotional Intelligence

Learning to Listen: I would count being a good listener as one of my stronger traits. I often prefer to let someone else speak first and then respond. However, the practice of dedicated active listening is a practice that felt relatively new to me, I guess it's more something I've never thought deeply about. I enjoyed the practice and can see how it will become very useful when working with a team and attempting to solve problems. It can be tempting to rush in with what you think might be the best solution, while instead actively listening to others views and solutions may yeild a more rounded and robust way to solve a problem.

Reading the Room: Over the years I've held down many odd jobs and also entertained crowds big and small with music. One of the most important things I have learned is the ability to be able to read the room and to respond appropriately to a given situation. When managing a stressful cafe I had to learn to control my emotions when the going got tough. The old adage "the customer is always right" often came to mind. Somtimes you really didn't want to remake a perfectly good coffee for a pedantic customer while in the middle of a busy rush, but being able to understand feelings of anger and frustration in a stressful situation, and to then supress those negative emotions and replace them with a smile and understanding can be the difference between a successful day and the wheels completely flying off.
