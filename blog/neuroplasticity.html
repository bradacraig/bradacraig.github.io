Neuroplasticity and Growth Mindset

Neuroplasticity is a way of retraining our brains to be more malleable when learning. It is directly related to how we practice and learn new skills by forming stronger pathways between neurons in the brain.

Practice makes permanent. The more times the network is stimulated, the stronger and more efficient it becomes.

By engaging in meaningful practice everyday we can increase our neuroplasticity.

The Benifits of Neuroplasticity and How We Can Engage With It

Once we understand the power and potential of neuroplasticity, we begin to remove the preconceptions that we are "not smart enough" or "too old" to learn and accept that meaningful, deep learning is absolutely possible at any stage of life.

One way I can engage with neuroplasticity for my own benefit is to engage in meaningful, regular practice. I have already begun this process in a small way by attempting to improve my typing skills with the hope of increasing my speed and accuracy. I never really learned to touch type properly and it has always bothered me, for a while I thought it may be a skill I might never actually pick up. Through my understanding of neuroplasticity and engaging in meaningful, regular practice every day I have already begun to see results. Little victories like this give me confidence that I will be able to learn more complex concepts throughout the Dev Academy course and hopefully throughout the rest of my life.

How Can We Increase Neuroplasticity?

Meaningful, regular practice helps strengthen existing pathways related to skills we already possess, while the same approach can help create new pathways to help us develop new skills or repair and recover from an injury or trauma.

In a similar vein, we can retrain our brain to have a more positive outlook simply by recognising negative thoughts and feelings when they arise and replacing them with positive ones. This can help give us confidence and can aid in further learning. If we don't encourage and strengthen the neuro pathways we want, we loose them. The inverse of this can also be true when developing bad habits and addictions.

Growth Mindset

A fixed mindset and a growth mindset are two contrasting beliefs about one's abilities and talents. A fixed mindset is the belief that intelligence and talents are static (fixed) and cannot be changed, leading to a defensive, negative, and defeatist approach when faced with challenges or failures. On the other hand, a growth mindset is the belief that intelligence and talents can be developed through effort and actions, promoting a positive, persevering, and inquisitive attitude towards learning.

These two mindsets are extremely valuable to understand when learning new skills as it can better help us to manage failures and setbacks by reminding us that we are able to switch up our mindsets to be more growth focused.

Exploring a Growth Mindset

While exploring these mindsets I found it interesting that pure belief in one's abilities can have a big effect on how we learn. It opens up possibilities and makes difficult tasks seem a lot more achievable.

I have definitely been guilty of slipping into a fixed mindset from time to time, particularly when it comes to how I let music and being a musician define my life. There was a point where I felt that I was unable to do anything else and that all other doors had closed. By adopting a growth mindset and convincing myself that I was still absolutely able to learn new skills I found the confidence to begin learning to code which has in turn led me to study at Dev Academy.

I will continue to integrate a growth mindset into my learning journey by embracing challenges, persisting in the face of setbacks, learning from mistakes and criticism and by finding lessons and inspiration in the success of others.
